🛡️ Palo Alto Networks: HR Diagnostic & Preventive Analytics System

An end-to-end HR analytics solution designed to shift organizational strategy from reactive attrition reporting to proactive employee well-being diagnostics. This project features a robust data engineering pipeline and an interactive Streamlit dashboard that empowers HR Business Partners to identify and support at-risk employees before they decide to leave.

🚀 Project Overview

In high-performance corporate environments, burnout frequently precedes attrition, and declining engagement silently erodes productivity. This project addresses these challenges by processing raw employee satisfaction and workload data to engineer advanced, composite Key Performance Indicators (KPIs). 

The resulting interactive dashboard provides leadership with real-time visibility into organizational health, career-stage stagnation, and workload stress, ultimately facilitating immediate, human-centered interventions.

✨ Key Features & Engineered KPIs

*   Unified Engagement Index: A composite metric aggregating normalized scores for Job Involvement, Job Satisfaction, Environment Satisfaction, and Relationship Satisfaction into a standard 0-100 scale.
*   Burnout Risk Stratification: A rigid, mathematically derived risk score (High/Medium/Low) based on the intersection of mandatory Overtime and Work-Life Balance deterioration.
*   Workload Stress Indicator: An additive index quantifying daily strain by mapping Business Travel intensity alongside an overtime penalty multiplier.
*   Satisfaction Stability Score: An out-of-the-box analytical concept measuring the standard deviation across an employee's satisfaction dimensions to identify highly volatile and erratic workplace experiences.
*   Manager Action Panel: A dynamic, row-highlighted intervention grid that distills thousands of rows into a direct "Priority Intervention List" for HR.
*   Automated Workflow Integration: Features an integrated, one-click copyable "1-on-1 Check-in Email Template" to seamlessly transition from data analysis to human intervention.

📊 Strategic Insights Discovered
The 3-Year Stagnation Cliff: Time-series tracking revealed sharp declines in engagement around Year 3 and Year 5, highlighting critical windows where employees feel biologically and professionally stagnant.

Overtime as an Engagement Detractor: Violin distributions confirmed that continuous, mandatory overtime shifts from being a productivity driver to an active engagement detractor.

Lateral Hires vs. Stagnation: The analysis successfully distinguished between normal lateral senior hires (low tenure, high level) and genuine career stagnation (high tenure, low level), ensuring high-risk cohorts were accurately targeted.
